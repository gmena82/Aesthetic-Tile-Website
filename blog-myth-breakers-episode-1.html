Costs 101: materials, labor, and waste (and how to control each)

Our floor-first budgeting philosophy

  • Plan the layout before day one. Center lines, doorways, and transitions matter.
  • Pick a grout that disappears. A close color match makes budget tile look premium.

  • Sheen matters. Glossy reflects light in smaller baths; matte reads calm and architectural.

Where premium pays off: accents that do the heavy lifting

Accents are the secret weapon in budget-wise design. Because they cover smaller areas, you can buy something special without inflating your total.

  • Mind transitions. Keep the plane changes tight and the edges clean‚Äîthis is where craftsmanship shows.

Common upgrade questions we get (and honest answers)

  • ‚ÄúWhat about grout‚Äîepoxy or cement?‚Äù Epoxy is stain-resistant and great for wet areas, but it‚Äôs pricier and trickier to install. High-quality cement grout with a sealer is still excellent for many projects. We‚Äôll advise based on your space and use.
  • ‚ÄúCan we go curbless?‚Äù Curbless showers look incredible and are easier to enter, but they require precise prep (recessed subfloor, proper slope, linear drain considerations). Budget more for this detail.
  • ‚ÄúDo I have to move plumbing to get the look I want?‚Äù Usually not. Keeping plumbing in place is one of the best ways to control costs. We‚Äôll design with your existing layout when it makes sense.
